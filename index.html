Day-Wise Themes

Your 5-Day Learning Journey

Each day builds progressively on the previous, creating a complete foundation in Ayurvedic culinary science.

Day 1

Introduction to Ayurvedic Food Thinking

Understanding food beyond taste

Begin your journey by shifting your relationship with food. We explore how Ayurveda sees food as a carrier of Prana — life energy — and why the quality, preparation, and mindset around cooking matters as much as the ingredients themselves.

  • The concept of Annam Brahma — food as the divine source
  • Sattvic, Rajasic, and Tamasic qualities of food
  • Why Ayurveda goes beyond calories and macronutrients
  • Hands-on: Preparing a simple Sattvic meal mindfully
Day 2

Understanding the Individual (Prakriti)

Why different people respond differently to the same food

No single diet works for everyone. Dive deep into the Tridosha system — Vata, Pitta, Kapha — and learn to identify your constitution through observable characteristics and self-assessment.

  • Detailed Prakriti self-assessment tool
  • Signs of Dosha imbalance (Vikriti) in daily life
  • Foods that heal vs. foods that aggravate each Dosha
  • Hands-on: Cooking dosha-specific dishes
Day 3

Taste & Food Basics (Rasa)

How taste influences the body

Ayurveda recognizes six tastes, each with distinct physiological effects on the Doshas, digestion, and mind. Learn to design meals that create deep cellular satisfaction and prevent cravings.

  • The six tastes: Sweet, Sour, Salty, Pungent, Bitter, Astringent
  • Post-digestive effect (Vipaka) of foods
  • How to correctly temper spices (Tadka) for maximum benefit
  • Hands-on: Creating a perfectly balanced six-taste thali
Day 4

Cooking Awareness & Food Behavior

What happens when food is cooked

The cook's intention and state of mind is absorbed into food. This day focuses on mindful cooking practices, food compatibility rules, and understanding how cooking transforms nutrients.

  • Food incompatibilities (Viruddha Ahara) to always avoid
  • How cooking methods affect Prana and nutrients
  • Mindful cooking: breathwork and presence in the kitchen
  • Hands-on: Prakriti-aware kitchen setup and meal prep
Day 5

Time, Seasonal Influence & Eating Awareness

Structuring food in daily life

Timing your meals with your body's natural rhythms is as important as what you eat. Learn to structure your daily eating routine and adapt your cooking seasonally.

  • Daily routine (Dinacharya) and meal timing
  • Seasonal eating guidelines (Ritucharya)
  • How to transition your home kitchen to Ayurvedic principles
  • Certification ceremony and personal commitment planning
